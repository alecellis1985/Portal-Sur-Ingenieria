    The energy policy in Uruguay was approved by the Executive Power in 2008 and it was agreed with all political parties with parliamentary representation in 2010. Uruguay has a state policy in the energy area and has several tax benefits.

    Mechanisms for the promotion of solar energy in Uruguay related to the Law No. 16.906 "Investment Promotion Law".

    The objective is the diversification of the energy matrix, both from sources and suppliers, seeking to reduce costs, reduce dependence on oil and seeking to encourage the participation of national energy sources, in particular renewable energies. This process will promote the transfer of technology and the development of national capacities and will seek to minimize the environmental impact of the sector.

    • The Federal Administration of Public Revenues (AFIP) implemented the procedure of accreditation and / or early refund of the Value Added Tax (VAT) to companies that use renewable sources for the production of electricity.
    • The companies included in the Promotion Regime for the use of renewable energy sources may simultaneously apply the Accelerated Depreciation of the assets foreseen in their investments in the Income Tax.
    • The General Resolution 4101 of the AFIP published in the Official Gazette, specifies that the benefit reaches the VAT that would have been billed to those responsible for the tax for the purchase, manufacture, production or final importation of capital assets, new in all cases, or the execution of infrastructure works, as provided for in decree 531 of March 30, 2016. In those cases, the tax "will be credited against other taxes collected by this Federal Administration, or otherwise it will be returned under the conditions that in this respect they are established ", says the regulation.
    • The Resolution of the AFIP, which is signed by Alberto Abad, recalls that the Promotion Regime institutes an investment regime for the construction of new works with the granting of promotional benefits, among them the early refund of the Value Added Tax

Main benefits of renewable energies

In the energetic subject we visualize 6 great subjects to stand out:

  1. Geopolitical: In the world, 92% of the energy consumed is focused on a few energy sources. This is why Uruguay and Argentina understand that it is vital to have renewable sources.
  2. Technological: Not only the primary energy is needed, but the technology is needed to transform it from primary to secondary energy and at the same time that needs to be useful.
  3. Economic: Given the growing global demand it is becoming increasingly expensive to obtain energy and technology, therefore the search for alternative energies is crucial.
  4. Ethical: 92% of the energies that are used worldwide are not renewable, this produces shortages in resources which should be handled in a more responsible manner.
  5. Environment: the production and use of energy are the main responsible for human emissions of carbon dioxide, the greatest impact on global climate change (60% of human carbon dioxide emissions are generated during production and the use of energy).
  6. Social: Access to energy is very unequal in the world and within each country, which is why a strong energy policy is important because it can be a powerful instrument to develop a country and promote social equality.
